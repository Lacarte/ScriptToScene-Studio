Every relationship has three stages.
Most people only experience the first two.
Stage one. Everything feels beautiful.
The texts feel exciting. The flaws feel cute.
You feel chosen. This is the stage where love feels easy.
Then comes stage two. This is where reality shows up.
You start noticing habits you ignored before.
Small things start to bother you.
Egos clash. Effort feels uneven.
This is the stage where most relationships end.
Not because love disappears,
but because it starts asking for patience instead of excitement.
But if two people choose to stay,
to listen instead of react,
to work together instead of walking away,
they reach stage three.
This is where love becomes calm.
Arguments don't turn into wars.
Silence doesn't feel scary.
You don't need to prove anything anymore.
You don't fall in love everyday.
You choose each other everyday.
And that's the stage people call real love.
